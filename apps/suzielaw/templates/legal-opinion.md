id: legal-opinion
title: Legal Opinion Letter
description: Layout for a formal legal opinion letter to a client or third party — addressee, scope of opinion, documents reviewed, assumptions, qualifications, opinion paragraphs, reliance language, signature block.
document_type: opinion-letter
when_to_use: Issuing a formal opinion of counsel — typically required at closing in financing or M&A transactions, or in response to a regulatory or audit request. Distinct from a memorandum: an opinion is *given* (not just analysis), is addressed to and relied upon by a specific party, and carries professional responsibility weight. Adjust the assumptions and qualifications to fit the transaction.
---

We have acted as [counsel to [PARTY] / special [STATE] counsel to [PARTY]] in connection with [the transaction / the matter] described above. This opinion letter is delivered to you pursuant to [Section [X] of the [Agreement] / your request].

## Documents Reviewed

In rendering the opinions set forth below, we have examined the following documents (collectively, the "**Reviewed Documents**"):

1. [Document 1, dated [DATE], between [PARTIES]];
2. [Document 2];
3. [Constituent documents — certificate of incorporation, bylaws, etc.];
4. [Resolutions, certificates of officers, good-standing certificates];
5. Such other documents and instruments as we have considered necessary or appropriate.

## Assumptions

In rendering the opinions set forth below, we have assumed, with your permission and without independent investigation:

(a) the genuineness of all signatures and the authenticity of all documents submitted to us as originals;
(b) the conformity to the originals of all documents submitted to us as copies;
(c) the legal capacity of all natural persons executing the Reviewed Documents;
(d) that each party to the Reviewed Documents (other than [PARTY]) is duly organized, validly existing, and has the power and authority to execute, deliver, and perform its obligations thereunder;
(e) that the Reviewed Documents have been duly authorized, executed, and delivered by each party thereto (other than [PARTY]) and constitute the legal, valid, and binding obligations of such party;
(f) [additional transaction-specific assumptions].

## Opinions

Based on the foregoing and subject to the qualifications set forth below, we are of the opinion that:

### 1. Due Organization

[PARTY] is a [corporation / limited liability company] duly [incorporated / organized], validly existing, and in good standing under the laws of the State of [STATE].

### 2. Power and Authority

[PARTY] has all requisite [corporate / limited liability company] power and authority to execute and deliver the [Agreement] and to perform its obligations thereunder.

### 3. Due Authorization, Execution, and Delivery

The execution and delivery of the [Agreement] by [PARTY] and the performance of its obligations thereunder have been duly authorized by all necessary [corporate / limited liability company] action on the part of [PARTY]. The [Agreement] has been duly executed and delivered by [PARTY].

### 4. Enforceability

The [Agreement] constitutes the legal, valid, and binding obligation of [PARTY], enforceable against it in accordance with its terms.

### 5. No Conflicts

The execution and delivery of the [Agreement] by [PARTY] and the performance by [PARTY] of its obligations thereunder do not (a) violate the [certificate of incorporation / bylaws] of [PARTY], (b) violate any [State] law or regulation applicable to [PARTY], or (c) [conflict with material agreements known to us — with appropriate carve-outs].

### 6. Additional Opinions

[Add additional opinion paragraphs as the transaction requires — e.g. securities exemptions, no governmental approvals required, etc.]

## Qualifications

The opinions set forth above are subject to the following qualifications:

### Bankruptcy Exception

Our opinion as to enforceability is subject to the effects of bankruptcy, insolvency, fraudulent conveyance, reorganization, moratorium, and other similar laws relating to or affecting creditors' rights generally.

### Equitable Principles

Our opinion as to enforceability is subject to general principles of equity, regardless of whether enforcement is sought in a proceeding at law or in equity.

### Specific Performance

We express no opinion regarding the availability of specific performance, injunctive relief, or other equitable remedies.

### Indemnification

We express no opinion as to the enforceability of indemnification, exculpation, or contribution provisions to the extent they purport to indemnify a party against the consequences of its own gross negligence, willful misconduct, or violation of public policy.

## Limitations

The opinions expressed herein are limited to (a) the laws of the State of [STATE] and (b) [the federal laws of the United States]. We express no opinion as to the laws of any other jurisdiction.

This opinion letter is rendered as of the date hereof, and we assume no obligation to update or supplement it to reflect facts or circumstances that may come to our attention or any changes in law that may occur after the date hereof.
