title: Court Brief
description: Layout for a litigation brief — caption, introduction, statement of facts, standard of review, argument (with point headings and IRAC sub-sections), conclusion, signature block, certificate of service.
document_type: brief
when_to_use: Filing a memorandum of law in support of or in opposition to a motion, or any merits brief. Tone is persuasive and one-sided — distinct from the neutral analysis of a memorandum. Adjust caption, parties, and case-number block to match the court's local rules.

## Preliminary Statement

[Open with one to three paragraphs stating, in plain English, (a) what relief the brief seeks, (b) the core reason the court should grant or deny it, and (c) the bottom-line outcome. This is the most important section — judges often read no further. Be concrete; avoid throat-clearing.]

## Statement of Facts

[Set out the relevant facts in chronological order. Cite to the record (e.g., "Compl. ¶ 14"; "Smith Decl. ¶ 3, Ex. A"; "Doc. 24-1 at 5") for every factual assertion. Order facts to support your narrative, but do not omit material facts unfavorable to your position — the court will find them.]

## Standard of Review

[State the legal standard governing the motion in one or two paragraphs, with citations. For example: "On a motion to dismiss under Rule 12(b)(6), the Court accepts all well-pleaded factual allegations as true and draws all reasonable inferences in the plaintiff's favor. *Iqbal*, 556 U.S. 662, 678 (2009)."]
