---
id: demand-letter
title: Demand Letter
description: Layout for a pre-litigation demand letter — addressee, factual background, legal basis, specific demand, deadline, reservation of rights, signature block.
document_type: letter
when_to_use: Asserting a claim and demanding action before filing suit. Tone is firm and specific. Distinct from a cease-and-desist (which demands stopping conduct without necessarily seeking damages) and from a settlement letter (which is part of an active dispute).
---

[DATE]

**[VIA CERTIFIED MAIL, RETURN RECEIPT REQUESTED, AND EMAIL]**

**[ADDRESSEE NAME]**
[ADDRESSEE TITLE / ENTITY]
[ADDRESS]

Re: **[CLIENT NAME] v. [ADDRESSEE NAME] — Demand for [PAYMENT / PERFORMANCE / CESSATION OF CONDUCT]**

Dear [MR./MS. ADDRESSEE]:

This firm represents [CLIENT LEGAL NAME] (our "**Client**") in connection with [the matter described below]. We write to formally demand [SUMMARY OF DEMAND — e.g. "payment of $[AMOUNT] in damages and immediate cessation of [CONDUCT]"]. This letter is sent in the hope of resolving this matter without resort to litigation; however, we are fully prepared to pursue all available legal remedies if a satisfactory resolution is not reached by the deadline set forth below.

## 1. Background

### Relationship of the Parties

[Briefly identify the parties and the relationship or transaction giving rise to the dispute — e.g., the contract, the engagement, the prior course of dealings.]

### Chronology

[Set out the facts giving rise to the claim in chronological order. Include dates, parties, and concrete actions. Stick to facts; save legal characterization for the next section.]

1. On [DATE], [our Client and the Addressee entered into [AGREEMENT]].
2. On [DATE], [the Addressee did/failed to do [ACTION]].
3. On [DATE], [our Client gave notice / made demand / suffered harm].

## 2. Legal Basis for the Claim

### Theory of Liability

[The Addressee's conduct constitutes [breach of contract / a violation of [STATUTE] / [TORT]]. Specifically, [section / element-level explanation, citing the specific contract provisions, statute, or cause of action].]

### Damages

[Describe the categories of damages — actual, consequential, statutory, restitution, fees — and the basis for each. Quantify where possible.]

## 3. Demand

We demand that, no later than [DEADLINE — typically 14 to 30 days from the date of the letter], you take the following actions:

### Required Actions

(a) **[Specific action — e.g. pay our Client the sum of $[AMOUNT] by wire transfer to the account designated below];**
(b) **[Specific action — e.g. cease and desist from [CONDUCT]];**
(c) **[Specific action — e.g. provide written confirmation of compliance];**
(d) [Additional demands as appropriate].

### Manner of Compliance

[How compliance must be evidenced — wire instructions, written certification, document delivery, etc.]

## 4. Consequences of Non-Compliance

If you fail to comply with this demand by [DEADLINE], our Client intends to pursue all available legal remedies, including but not limited to filing suit for [CAUSES OF ACTION] and seeking [damages, injunctive relief, attorneys' fees, and costs] as permitted by [the [Agreement] / applicable law]. [Statutory fee-shifting or multiplier provisions, if applicable.]

## 5. Preservation of Evidence

You are hereby on notice of a likely dispute and are required to preserve all documents, communications, and electronically stored information (including emails, messages, and metadata) potentially relevant to this matter. Failure to preserve such evidence may result in spoliation sanctions.

## 6. Reservation of Rights

Nothing in this letter constitutes a waiver of any of our Client's rights, claims, or remedies, all of which are expressly reserved. This letter is sent for purposes of [Federal Rule of Evidence 408 and equivalent state rules] as a settlement communication.

We invite you to contact the undersigned promptly to discuss resolution. We hope this matter can be addressed without litigation, but our Client will not hesitate to enforce its rights if necessary.
